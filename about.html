About Bunista Sacco Society Ltd

Bunista Sacco Society Ltd was registered on 24th November 2010 under the Ministry of Co-operative Development and operates within Jaramogi Oginga Odinga University. Its main objective is to organize and promote the welfare and economic interest of members.

RegistrationRegistered under Co-operative Societies Act Cap 490, Registration No. 12880.
Membership BaseJOOUST employees and their spouses within a trusted common bond.
LocationMain campus, Finance Office Compound behind Procurement Offices.
📜

History

The Sacco was established to serve members through structured savings, prudent lending, and cooperative support. Over time, it has steadily built a credible presence around member-focused financial empowerment.

🎯

Main Purpose

To organize and promote the welfare and economic interest of members through a cooperative model that values thrift, shared growth, and responsible access to credit.

📈

Growth Journey

Since inception, Bunista has experienced meaningful growth in membership, share capital, and non-withdrawable deposits, strengthening its stability and relevance.

Objectives

1

To encourage thrift among members and build a savings culture that supports financial stability.

2

To ensure personal growth through products and services that promote the economic and social base of members.

3

To educate members on proper use of credit, eradicate poverty, uphold human dignity, and encourage cooperation.

Universal Co-operative Principles

The values that guide Bunista

1
Voluntary and open membership

A welcoming model founded on fairness and access within the common bond.

2
Democratic member control

Members participate in decision-making and shape the direction of the Sacco.

3
Economic participation by members

Members contribute, save, and benefit from the cooperative structure.

4
Autonomy and independence

The Sacco maintains its identity and serves members with accountability.

5
Education, training and information

Continuous learning helps members make sound financial decisions.

6
Co-operation among co-operatives

Collective strength supports broader growth and resilience.

7
Concern for community

The cooperative spirit extends beyond individuals to the wider community.
